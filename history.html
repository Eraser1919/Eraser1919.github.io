The history of medical technology can go back very far where instruments were used for surgery, amputation… However, this will only cover some of the major breakthroughs starting from 1816 based on the Yale Medicine Magazine.

1816

In 1816, the first Stethoscope was invented by RenéLaennec, MD.

1851

The Ophthalmoscope was invented by Hermann von Helmholtz in 1851.

1895

In 1895, X-rays were discovered by Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen, PhD. The first X-ray image in the US was published and a clinical x-ray was taken a month later.

1903

In 1903, Willem Einthoven, MD, refined the electrocardiogram for recording heart electrical impulses.

1924

In 1924, a German physiologist and psychiatrist, Hans Berger, MD, developed electroencephalography which records brain waves through the skull.

1929

In 1929, Philip Drinker invented the iron lung which helped polio patients breathe.

1931

The first electron microscope was designed by Max Knoll and Ernst Ruska in 1931.

1937

In 1937, the first artificial heart was made by Vladimir Demikhov, and transplanted into a dog, but it only survived for 2 hours.

1943

The first artificial kidney was made by Willem J. Kolff, MD, PhD, in 1943 which evolved into the modern-day dialysis machine.

1947-1950

In 1947, the Transistor, a semiconductor device, was invented by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley which eventually led to the development of cardiac pacemakers by John Hopps in 1950.

1951

Paul Zoll, MD, developed an external tabletop pacemaker that treats hearts blocks.

1960

In 1960, Theodore H. Maiman, PhD, operated the first optical laser. Lasers can be used for surgery, ophthalmoscopy, kidney stone, cosmetic skin treatments, and more.

1971

In 1971, Paul Lauterbur, PhD, applied magnetic field gradients to nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) images. With Peter Mansfield, PhD, they developed MRI techniques like echo-planar imaging.

1975

In 1975, Robert S. Ledley, MS, DDS, created a whole-body computed tomography (CT) scan.

1977

The first full body MRI scanner was constructed in 1977 by Raymond Damadian, MD.

1978

In 1978, William V. Tamborlane, MD, and Robert S. Sherwin, MD created an insulin infusion pump for diabetes which improved control of insulin levels in children.
